Montreal

Saint-Henri

Savsav is a fun hidden coffee shop. Go in through the side door near the parking. Cafe Mina Mina is good for a quick cup to go (and easier to find!). Luncheonette has great food but I don't like the coffee. A good pick for brunch. The avocado toast at September Cafe is great.

Arthur's and l'Avenue are the two well loved brunch places nearby but beware of lines. I've never been to either for that reason. I usually prefer Schnitzel & Cie par Bagel St-Henri. The owner is friendly.

Velora has amazing desserts and a cute mediterranean style fountain.

Dalla Rose and La Diperie are two nearby yummy ice cream shops. The flavors at Dalla Rose are creative which can be fun but also occasionally strange - I recommend sampling them before committing to a flavor.

To try poutine, you'll have many options. My favorite cheap pick in the neighborhood is from St Henri Pizza.

For more delicious pizza : I love Pizza Toni. Elena is on my list but never tried it yet. Nearby is Sidney's thrift store (amazing) and Bernice (amazing cookies).

Notre-Dame-du-Vintage is a vintage store with lots of different rooms - lots of fun to explore the different themed spaces, like a witchy room and an MCM Tiki style room.

Saint-Henri Books is a great local bookstore. It's near Dalla Rose and La Diperie, so it pairs well with an ice cream stop.

There's lots of Mexican places in st Henri. My favorite is Tacos Frida. Try the El Flaco tacos, the quesadillas, and the aguas frescas - especially the hibiscus one (agua de Jamaica). The birria from Tacos Tin Tan is also great.

For sushis, a local favorite is Kioko.

For middle eastern, Sumac is a local fav. The fast food pick would be boustan, though the prices have really gone up in recent years. For something upscale, HENI is fine dining and was delicious in a gentle atmosphere.

For Indian Khao Peeyo is nearby and great for takeout.

For wine, Vin Papillon is lovely. So is Barbara if you'd also like to try their cacio e pepe. They're also good for coffee in the morning - the pistachio iced latte is great.

For beer, there's many local breweries. A local favorite is Messorem, a large open space near the bike path. Siboire is also great and nearby.

For cocktails, Atwater Cocktail club has a nice upscale atmosphere. For a more casual atmosphere Pub Epoxy is a welcoming dive bar.

Canal lounge is a bar on a cute boat. Very nice atmosphere even for non drinkers. Pairs nicely with boat or SUP rentals right across the nearby bridge.

The closest supermarket is IGA, but it is more expensive than super C, which is not too far. They both have parking lots. Atwater Market has a better selection of local goods.

For any quick supplies, Dollarama is nearby. Amazing dollar store (though nothing is a dollar anymore sadly).

For a drugstore, there's Jean Coutu.

Verdun

I love the Verdun neighborhood. There's bouldering at Allez Up, many cute cafes like Lili et Oli, and the Verdun Beach. It gets less crowded if you go left of the beach to swim (not official and no lifeguard - less crowds and same water).

For Indian, a delicious casual dine in experience we loved Palais D'Ajit.

Griffintown & Old Port

Other adjacent neighborhoods include Griffintown and the Old Port. The old Port in particular is very cute old buildings and has lots of touristic attractions.

Cirque du soleil is a great show & overall experience! I've been many times with my mom.

Saute-Moutons for thrills, but skip La Ronde unless you're desperate for a roller coaster. If interested in fireworks, La Ronde does them in the summers every Thursday and Sunday from early July to early August. Watch them from the Jacques-Cartier Bridge (closed to cars on show nights so you can walk right out on it) or one of the bridges, but be aware there will be crowds.

There's also a spa on a boat, Bota Bota, which is nice and peaceful (but very quiet). If that's your kind of thing.

For Indian, India Rosa is the more upscale choice with a nice atmosphere.

Rockaberry for dessert, Arté for vintage finds, and Idolem for hot yoga.

Downtown

Great chocolate dipped ice cream at Chocolats Favoris. Try it while visiting place des arts area.

Homers Donuts is a local donut chain with rotating menu and big fresh donuts!

For a nicer dinner, I recommend Les Enfants Terribles for its view. Bar George has a really swanky original 1800s architecture. It's near the Musée des Beaux-Arts, which would pair nicely.

For take in, Kinton ramen is delicious and has clever packaging to keep it fresh.

If you want all you can eat sushi, Manga Bistro has a fun decor and is yummy.

For shopping, Simons.

Westmount

I love Gentile Pizza Parlor.

Bagels On Greene is also great, and also offers grocery items.

Mont Royal & the Plateau

Another classic is mount Royal. A hike up the mountain pairs great with the Plateau neighborhood. You can also see some great shops and restaurants there.

Beaver Lake is a great walk and is close to a few different lookouts for wonderful views.

Sushi Momo has great vegetarian sushis.

Blue Boy for ice cream.

For Ethiopian, Queen Sheba. The best dish is actually the vegetarian appetizer platter - it's large enough for a meal and the tastiest option. Their house red is nice, too.

Mile End

For vegetarian eats, the veggie pulled pork from Dep Le pickup in the Mile end neighborhood is amazing. Cafe Even nearby is great. Try their blueberry cold foam. Falafel Yoni is another great veggie pick. There's a few locations.

Park Angrignon

Park Angrignon has a large lake with ducks that you can walk around. Pairs nicely with a visit to the classic Dilallo Burger or Poulet Fou et Frit (mom and pop fried chicken place with karaoke machine and massage chairs!)

Further out / rainy day

For sushis in general my favorite is Kyoto sushi, though it's a bit far.

For a rainy day, the new Royal Mount Mall has high end shopping, as well as a great food court and new arcade. The biodôme is also quite impressive. It features replicas of 4 different ecosystems from plants to weather to animals - immersive and educational!

My favorite water park nearby is the one in Saint-Sauveur where you literally go down the river.

Getting around

Bixi (bike share) stations are everywhere. Unlock with the Bixi app, single ride or a day pass. The Lachine canal path is the prettiest ride.

For metro and bus, grab an OPUS card ($6) from any metro station (agent or vending machine, also some pharmacies/deps) and load at least one fare. Reload at the machines, or tap the card on your phone in the Chrono app. For a short visit the single-use L'Occasionnelle card (1-trip, 24h or 3-day) is simpler.

For street parking, the city app is Mobicité. Free, about 7¢ per transaction, with a 10-min end-of-session reminder. You can't extend a session, just start a new one when it runs out. For lots and garages, ClicknPark lets you find and book a spot ahead by the hour, day, or month.
